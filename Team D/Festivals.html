Festivals in Cambodia

Cambodia is known for its vibrant festivals that reflect its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the most celebrated festivals in the country:

International New Year

Cambodians celebrate the global New Year with family gatherings, fireworks, and city events. Many people visit public parks, pagodas, or travel to the countryside to welcome the year with positivity and good wishes. Although not a traditional Cambodian festival, it has become widely celebrated.

Victory Over Genocide Day

This day marks the fall of the Khmer Rouge regime in 1979. Ceremonies are held across the country to honor victims and acknowledge the liberation. Schools, government officials, and citizens attend remembrance events to reflect on peace and healing.

Meak Bochea

A major Buddhist holiday held on the full moon of the third lunar month. It commemorates the day when 1,250 enlightened monks gathered to hear Buddha preach. Cambodians go to pagodas for candlelight processions, prayers, and offerings, reflecting on moral discipline and kindness.

International Women’s Day

Celebrated nationwide to honor women’s contributions to society. Schools and organizations hold events promoting gender equality, while families show appreciation for the women in their lives.

Khmer New Year

The most important traditional festival in Cambodia. It marks the end of the harvest season and the start of a new year. Families reunite, clean their homes, visit pagodas, offer prayers, and play traditional games like chol chhoung and bos angkunh. Streets are filled with music, dance, and joyful celebrations.

Visak Bochea

One of Buddhism's holiest days, celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Buddha. Cambodians visit pagodas for meditation, merit-making, and candlelight ceremonies. It’s a time for spiritual reflection and practicing good deeds.

Royal Ploughing Ceremony

A symbolic royal ceremony marking the start of the rice planting season. Two royal oxen plough the ceremonial field, then choose from bowls of grain, water, and crops. Their choices are believed to predict rainfall, harvest quality, and the year’s agricultural fortune.

International Labour Day

A public holiday recognizing workers and their contributions to society. Many workers have the day off, and some organizations host events supporting labor rights and fair working conditions.

King Norodom Sihamoni’s Birthday

A national celebration honoring the King. Cultural programs, charity activities, and official ceremonies are held in Phnom Penh near the Royal Palace. Citizens express gratitude for the King's leadership and peaceful guidance.

Queen Mother’s Birthday

This day honors the Queen Mother Norodom Monineath Sihanouk. Media broadcasts highlight her charitable contributions, and celebrations take place around the Royal Palace with decorations and traditional performances.

Constitutional Day

Commemorates the signing of the 1993 Constitution, which restored the monarchy and democracy in Cambodia. Schools and institutions hold events to raise awareness of citizens' rights and responsibilities.

Pchum Ben

A 15-day religious festival where families honor ancestors. People visit pagodas early in the morning to offer food to monks, believing prayers and offerings help send blessings to departed relatives. It is one of Cambodia’s most spiritual and meaningful traditions.

King Father Norodom Sihanouk’s Memorial Day

A day to honor the late King Father’s life and contributions to Cambodia. Citizens pay respect, and the Royal Palace holds official ceremonies. Many reflect on his influence in shaping modern Cambodia.

Paris Peace Agreement Day

Marks the signing of the 1991 Paris Peace Agreements, which ended years of conflict and led to national reconciliation. Schools and organizations highlight the importance of peace and unity.

King Sihamoni’s Coronation Day

Celebrates the coronation of King Norodom Sihamoni in 2004. The day features official ceremonies, televised broadcasts, and cultural performances dedicated to the King.

Water Festival

One of the largest festivals in Cambodia, celebrating the reversing flow of the Tonle Sap River. Phnom Penh becomes alive with long-boat races, illuminated river floats, concerts, and fireworks. It represents gratitude for water, agriculture, and unity.

Independence Day

Marks Cambodia’s independence from France in 1953. The main ceremony takes place at the Independence Monument in Phnom Penh, attended by the King and government officials. Parades, flags, and nationwide celebrations symbolize patriotism and national pride.
