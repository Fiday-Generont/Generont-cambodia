Discover Cambodia’s Ancient Wonders

Explore the timeless beauty of Angkor Wat, Bayon, Ta Prohm, and other historic temples that reflect the glory of the Khmer Empire.

Explore Now

Angkor Wat Temple

Angkor Wat is Cambodia most iconic temple, built in the 12th century by King Suryavarman II. Originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu, it later became a Buddhist temple. The complex is massive, featuring grand towers, long galleries, and detailed bas-reliefs depicting Hindu mythology. It is the largest religious monument in the world and draws visitors from across the globe, especially at sunrise, when the temple’s reflection in surrounding moats creates a stunning visual. Angkor Wat represents the pinnacle of Khmer architecture and remains a powerful symbol of Cambodia’s history, religion, and national identity.

Bayon Temple

Bayon Temple, located in the center of Angkor Thom, is famous for its numerous serene stone faces carved into its towers. Constructed in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it blends Buddhist and Hindu symbolism. The temple’s bas-reliefs vividly depict scenes of ancient Khmer life, including battles, markets, and daily activities, offering historical insight. Bayon’s central location and unique architectural style make it one of the most visited temples in Angkor. Its mysterious smiling faces, known as the “faces of Avalokiteshvara,” give the temple a distinct character that fascinates historians, artists, and tourists alike.

Ta Prohm Temple

Ta Prohm is famous for the enormous trees and massive roots that intertwine with its ruins, creating a striking jungle temple. Built in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it served as a Buddhist monastery and university. Unlike other restored temples, Ta Prohm has been intentionally left in a partially ruined state to preserve its natural charm. Its combination of ancient stone structures and twisting roots provides a cinematic atmosphere, famously featured in the movie Tomb Raider. Visitors come to explore its mysterious corridors, massive stone carvings, and the unique experience of nature reclaiming human architecture.

Banteay Srei Temple

Banteay Srei is a small temple renowned for its exquisite pink and red sandstone carvings. Dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva, it was constructed in the 10th century and is often called the “Citadel of Women” because of the delicacy of its intricate carvings. Unlike larger Angkorian temples, Banteay Srei emphasizes detailed sculptural decoration over sheer size, featuring elaborate floral motifs, mythological scenes, and divine figures. Its carvings have survived centuries with remarkable clarity. The temple is set in a peaceful countryside location, attracting visitors who appreciate art, history, and the unique craftsmanship of the Khmer civilization.

Preah Vihear Temple is perched atop a 525-meter cliff along the Cambodia–Thailand border, offering breathtaking views. Built primarily between the 9th and 12th centuries, it is dedicated to Shiva and showcases classical Khmer architectural techniques. The temple’s long processional stairways, intricate carvings, and well-planned layout highlight its religious and strategic importance. Preah Vihear has been the center of territorial disputes but is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its historical, cultural, and architectural value. Its remote location and dramatic mountain setting make it a remarkable destination for history enthusiasts and adventurous travelers..
